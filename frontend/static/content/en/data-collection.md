#### Example

If a participant consumes 200ml of low-calorie sports drink plus a 180kcal gel at a certain point, they should mark:

- Water: 2 portions
- Electrolytes: 1 portion (depending on drink and gel content, could be 2)
- Food: 2 portions

### Rate of Perceived Exertion (RPE)

For practical purposes, we use a simplified 5-level scale instead of 10:

- **1-2**: Minimal activity (resting)
- **3-4**: Very light activity (very easy walk or jog)
- **5-6**: Moderate activity (trending to strong). Competition pace for long-duration activity.
- **7-8**: Very high activity. High pace that could only be sustained for a few minutes.
- **9-10**: Maximum possible effort. Duration of a few seconds.

### App Controls During Activity

- **START Button**: Add (+1) to selected item / Increase RPE
- **BACK Button**: Undo (-1) from selected item / Decrease RPE
- **UP/DOWN Buttons**: Cycle through data fields (RPE, Water, Electrolytes, Food, MENU)

1. Very bad
2. Bad
3. Normal

## Upload Data

Once the activity is complete, sync your Garmin device and upload the .fit file through the Trail Fit Uploader platform, along with the contextual information requested.
